New York City spread half a million tons of road salt last winter

A decade of data shows the city is using more salt every year.

Each truck icon represents 500 salt delivery trucks, with each vehicle carrying 22 tons of salt.

The 2015–2016 season saw 266,265 tons of road salt spread across city streets, a record high tracked by the Department of Sanitation.

In five years the amount jumped 70%

By the 2020–21 season, the city was using 453,763 tons. Heavier snowfall and growing road network both play a role.

Over half a million tons
